title: Bed Management in a Critical Care Unit

**Abstract:** One of the main problems facing hospital managers is in coping with the
variability in demand for the services that the hospital provides. This is
particularly the case in the Critical Care Unit (CCU), where inability to
provide adequate facilities on demand can lead to serious consequences.
Admissions to CCUs may be categorized under two headings: unplanned (emergency)
and planned (elective). The length of stay (LoS) in the CCU is heavily dependent
on the admission category: unplanned admissions have a much longer LoS on
average than elective patients. In this paper, we propose a mathematical model
that shows how improvements in bed management may be achieved by distinguishing
between these two categories of patients. The vast majority of previous
literature in this field is concerned only with steady-state conditions, whereas
in reality, activities in virtually all hospital environments are very much time
dependent. This paper goes some way to addressing this problem.
