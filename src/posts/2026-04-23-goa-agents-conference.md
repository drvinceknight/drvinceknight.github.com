title: Visiting India for the Cardiff Agents Conference
---

I am writing this on my way back home after spending a wonderful 3 days at the
Cardiff University Agent Conference. The purpose of this conference is to build
relationships with the various agents that help students in India apply to
University.

I was asked to attend and show the newest masters programme we are offering at
the School of Mathematics:
[Artificial Intelligence and Statistical Analytics
(MSc)](https://www.cardiff.ac.uk/study/postgraduate/taught/courses/course/artificial-intelligence-and-statistical-analytics-msc).

This was my first trip to India so I was delighted at the opportunity.

Upon arrival I made sure to keep my bribentives safe for the session I was
running the next day.

![bribentives](/assets/static/2026-04-23-goa-agents-conference/bribentives_in_safe.jpeg)

had a number of team building exercises which were a bunch of fun and a nice way

![War cry](/assets/static/2026-04-23-goa-agents-conference/judging_panel.jpeg)

It is always somewhat amusing, how far we have to go from Cardiff to find out
what great work our colleagues do. Or even to meet them! In one particular case
to meet them again, I was sat on the judging panel with
[Eleri](https://profiles.cardiff.ac.uk/staff/rosiere): we had met about
15 years ago doing our Postgraduate Teaching Certificate and not seen each other
since. It was a real pleasure to hang out again.

In the evening we had a beach party.

![Beach party](/assets/static/2026-04-23-goa-agents-conference/beach_party.jpeg)

On the second day I ran my game theory workshop where I walk students through a
replication of [Axelrod's tournament](https://vknight.org/posts/2025-10-21-rrr/)
to understand how game theory can be used to model emergence of cooperation
through direct reciprocity. This lead to me talking about our new MSc. The
councillors were fantastic sports and made the session fun:

![](/assets/static/2026-04-23-goa-agents-conference/defection_3.jpeg)

The full set of results is here, this was a particularly uncooperative

In the afternoon [Reece](https://profiles.cardiff.ac.uk/staff/lewisr74) ran an
absolutely brilliant session where he asked students to argue a legal case. He
was the judge and here is my genuine reaction the first time he spoke quite
sternly (in judge mode) to one of the "barristers".

![](/assets/static/2026-04-23-goa-agents-conference/reaction_to_judge_mode.jpeg)

It was brilliant and
incredibly
thought provoking too (the case he had them argue is apparently a classic one
that set a precedent of [necessity not being a defence against murder](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/R_v_Dudley_and_Stephens).).

This was a brilliant event and a privileged to be part of. Meeting all the
councillors and learning from them as to what we can do better for Indian
students was my highlight. I feel like I also made a lot of friends.
