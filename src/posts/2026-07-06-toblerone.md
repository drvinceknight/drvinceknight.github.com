title: "Is a Toblerone a Bar? A Rigorous Treatment of Segmented Confections"
---

Harry Foster and I have written a paper. I maintain that a Toblerone is a
chocolate bar. Harry maintains that it is not. We were unable to resolve
this.

The paper is a joint effort in the sense that we share an axiom system and
disagree entirely about what follows from it. The first result (mine) proves
the Toblerone is a bar. The second result (Harry's) proves it is a segmented
confection homeomorphic to a box of chocolates. We then prove these two
results are mutually incompatible, model the dispute as a game, and show it
has no pure-strategy Nash equilibrium. Claude is listed as a third author;
it declined the honour and was added anyway.

The one thing all three authors agree on: the Terry's Chocolate Orange is
horrid. This is Corollary 4, and it is unanimous.
